Introduction / About the Issue

Protecting the planet is essential for sustaining life on Earth. Human activities have caused climate change, pollution, deforestation, and loss of biodiversity — all threatening the balance of our ecosystems.

By understanding these problems and taking action, we can help preserve our home for future generations.

Why It Matters

Key environmental problems:

  • Climate Change: Rising temperatures cause extreme weather, ice melt, and sea-level rise.
  • Pollution: Air, water, and soil pollution harm health and wildlife.
  • Deforestation: Trees are cut down faster than they regrow, reducing oxygen and habitats.
  • Loss of Biodiversity: Species extinction disrupts ecosystems and food chains.

Quick facts:

  • The last decade was the warmest on record.
  • Over 8 million tons of plastic enter oceans yearly.
  • More than 15 billion trees lost each year globally.

Easy Tips to Help

  • Use less plastic – bring reusable bags & bottles.
  • Turn off lights and unplug devices when not in use.
  • Recycle paper, plastic, glass, and metals properly.
  • Plant a tree or support reforestation projects.
  • Walk, bike, or use public transport instead of driving.

Encourage sustainable living: use renewable energy, buy eco-friendly products, and reduce waste.

Local and Global Initiatives

Some organizations and agreements working to protect the environment:

  • World Wildlife Fund (WWF) – Protecting endangered species and habitats.
  • Greenpeace – Environmental activism worldwide.
  • The Paris Agreement – Global pact to limit climate change.

Look for local events like clean-up drives or tree-planting campaigns in your community and join in!
